Our work in Mankosi building a Solar Community Hub

Computer Aid has completed a project in Mankosi, South Africa, deploying a Solar Community Hub infrastructure, set to open soon. In partnership with Utelize Mobile, Mobliciti, Dell Technologies, Intel, and Zenzeleni Networks, the hub uses recycled shipping containers to create a self-sufficient space with solar panels and computer technology, providing a working space for people in remote areas.

Laptops for Schools

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Computer Aid launched an appeal for laptop donations to support online learning for children without access to technology at home. The appeal was highly successful, with large donations from companies, including some covering costs for collection, processing, and distribution. Notably, Avanade, a joint venture between Microsoft and Accenture, partnered with Computer Aid to purchase laptops for schools in disadvantaged areas in the UK, aligning with their corporate social responsibility policies.
